# SimCity 2000 Clone: Feature Investigation & Backlog

This document organizes the core features of SimCity 2000 into a structured backlog, grouped by Minimum Viable Products (MVPs) to ensure a progressive and testable development cycle.

## Global Architectural Constraints
- **Master Configuration:** NO MAGIC NUMBERS. All simulation weights, costs, thresholds, and variables must be stored in a centralized master balancing `config.js` (or similar) to ensure the game is fully tunable from a single source of truth.

## Investigation of SimCity 2000 Mechanics

### 1. Zoning & Density
- **Zones:** Residential (R), Commercial (C), Industrial (I).
- **Density:** Light and Dense.
- **Growth Engine:** Buildings emerge based on demand (RCI), land value, power, and later, water and transportation access.
- **Sizes:** Structures range from 1x1 up to 3x3 tiles, dynamically merging or splitting.

### 2. Utilities
- **Power:** Power plants have distinct outputs, costs, and lifespans. Power automatically travels along road networks to adjacent zoned buildings.
- **Water:** Water pumps/towers supply water. Water automatically travels along road networks. Water is necessary for dense development.

### 3. Transportation
- **Roads & Highways:** Network for statistical traffic trips between R, C, and I zones. Intersections increase congestion metrics.
- **Rail & Subway:** Mass transit reduces road traffic.
- **Ports & Airports:** Required for heavy industrial growth and late-game commercial demand.

### 4. City Services & Civic Buildings
- **Safety:** Police (reduces crime), Fire stations (puts out fires).
- **Education & Health:** Schools, Colleges, Libraries, Museums (boost educational quotient), Hospitals (boost life expectancy).
- **Recreation:** Parks, Zoos, Stadiums, Marinas (boost land value and happiness).
- **Rewards:** City Hall, Statue (milestones).

### 5. Deep Simulation Dimensions
- **RCI Demand Tracker:** Macro-level economic simulation.
- **Land Value:** Influenced by terrain (water/trees), pollution, crime, and distance to city center/services.
- **Pollution:** Generated by industry and traffic; decreases land value.
- **Crime:** Generated by population density and low land value; suppressed by police coverage.
- **City Budget:** Taxes (adjustable by RCI), bonds, funding levels for services/transit.

### 6. Terrain & Environment
- Top-down view with flat 2D terrain.
- Mountains and water are represented by flat un-buildable obstacle tiles (colored/sprites).
- No complex multi-layer height rendering. Bridges or simple tunnels will act as traversable connections over/under these obstacles.

## Proposed MVP Backlog

### MVP 1: The Core Loop (Micropolis Baseline)
*Focus: Get the engine to simulate basic growth without overly complex dependencies.*
- [x] **Grid & Camera:** Basic 2D grid rendering, panning, zooming.
- [x] **Zoning Tools:** Ability to paint Light R, C, and I zones.
- [x] **Power Simulation:** Place a power plant; implement power propagation automatically along road networks. Include SC2000 shortage logic (rolling brownouts, flashing power symbols, eventual building abandonment).
- [x] **Basic Growth:** RCI zones develop 1x1 or 2x2 buildings if powered and connected to basic roads.
- [x] **Time & Demand:** Simple ticker. Rudimentary RCI demand formula (e.g., people need jobs -> I/C demand; jobs need people -> R demand).

### MVP 2: Advanced Utilities & Advanced Zoning
*Focus: Deepening the requirements for higher-level building growth.*
- [x] **Dense Zoning:** Add Dense R, C, I zoning options.
- [x] **Water System:** Implement water pumps and automatic water propagation along roads. Include SC2000 shortage logic (dense buildings flash water symbols and abandon if dry).
- [x] **Building Merging:** Algorithm to spawn 3x3 structures under high land value, dense zoning, and water supply.
- [x] **Bulldozer Tool:** Clearing terrain and destroying buildings.

### MVP 3: Desirability, Services, & Land Value
*Focus: Giving the simulation a soul (happiness, safety, and health).*
- [ ] **Pollution Map:** Industry and traffic generate pollution; pollution decays over time/distance.
- [ ] **Crime Map & Police:** Proximity-based crime generation; Police Station radius mitigates it.
- [ ] **Fire & Fire Stations:** Random fire outbreaks (based on power/density); Fire Station radius suppresses fires.
- [ ] **Land Value Calculation:** A matrix combining pollution, crime, terrain features, and center-of-mass proximity.
- [ ] **Civic Buildings Tool:** Place Parks, Schools, and Hospitals to manually boost land value.

### MVP 4: Transportation & Advanced Networks
*Focus: Movement around the city.*
- [ ] **Traffic Simulation:** Macro-statistical traffic heat map (congestion based on zone density, trip calculations, and road capacity, not individual agents).
- [ ] **Highways & Interchanges:** Higher capacity traffic routes that reduce statistical congestion.
- [ ] **Mass Transit:** Rail and train stations to reduce localized road traffic.

### MVP 5: Economy, Budget, and Government
*Focus: The meta-game and UI overlays.*
- [ ] **Budget System:** Deep yearly financial simulation (individual R, C, I tax rates, fine-tuned funding sliders for fire/police/transit which dynamically impact their effectiveness).
- [ ] **Data Maps Tool:** Visual overlays for Crime, Pollution, Land Value, Power, Water.
- [ ] **The "Sim" Feedback:** Newspaper UI or ticker to show resident complaints.
- [ ] **Bonds & Loans:** Financial instruments.

### MVP 6: The "SC2000" Polish
*Focus: What makes SC2000 iconic.*
- [ ] **Bridges & Obstacles:** Logic for roads/power mapping across water and mountain obstacle tiles.
- [ ] **Ports & Airports:** Specialized massive zones for industrial/commercial caps.
- [ ] **Subways:** Dual-layer network (surface + underground subways).
- [ ] **Disasters:** Optional toggleable disruptive events (fires, earthquakes).

### MVP 7: Nice-To-Haves & Expansions
*Focus: Features to implement aggressively late in development.*
- [ ] **Neighbor Connections:** Map edge connections that simulate external trade, traffic, and loosely boost demand.
- [ ] **City Ordinances:** Enactable policies (e.g., smoke detectors, parking fines) that cost money but affect simulation variables like crime, health, or traffic.
